“Dressed in new jeans, a light blue dress shirt and a red patterned tie, he stood at Heathers grave with his eyes closed.
Although I didnt hear him, his lips were moving like he was praying.
In the faint breeze, Mother Nature ran her fingers through his dark hair like I wanted to.
He looked tall and strong, the way he used to, but somewhere along the way, without me, hed stepped into the shoes of a man.
And a part of me ached for those missing years.”
